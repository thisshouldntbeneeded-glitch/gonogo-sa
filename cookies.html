2. What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit. They allow the website to remember information about your visit — such as your preferences, login state, or browsing behaviour — and can be read back during future visits.

Similar technologies include mechanisms that achieve comparable results without using a traditional cookie file. We use the following on the Website:

  • localStorage — a browser-based storage area that persists data on your device with no automatic expiry. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is not sent to the server with every HTTP request; it is accessed only by client-side JavaScript. localStorage is subject to the same disclosure obligations as cookies under South African law.
  • HTTP cookies — set by web servers or third-party scripts and transmitted with every matching HTTP request. These may be session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (stored for a defined period).

Cookies and similar technologies can be first-party (set by us) or third-party (set by an external service such as Google Analytics).

7. How to Manage and Control Cookies

You have several ways to manage the cookies and similar technologies used on this Website.

7.1 Via Our Cookie Banner

The simplest way to change your consent choice is to clear your browser's localStorage for www.gonogo.co.za. This will cause the cookie banner to appear again on your next visit, allowing you to make a new choice.

7.2 Via Your Browser Settings

All modern browsers allow you to view, manage, and delete cookies and localStorage data. The steps vary by browser:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data. You can clear cookies for all sites or for gonogo.co.za specifically. To access localStorage, open DevTools (F12) → Application → Local Storage.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Manage Data. For localStorage, open DevTools (F12) → Storage → Local Storage.
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data.
  • Apple Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data.
  • Mobile browsers: Settings → Privacy or Site Settings for your specific browser application.

Blocking strictly necessary localStorage items (such as gonogo_theme and gonogo_cookie_consent) may affect the Website's appearance and functionality — for example, the consent banner will be shown on every visit and your theme preference will not be remembered.

7.3 Opt Out of Google Analytics

In addition to using the cookie banner on our Website, you can prevent Google Analytics from collecting your data across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-On.

You can also manage Google's use of your data via Google's My Account settings.

7.4 Do Not Track

Some browsers support a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. We acknowledge DNT signals and, where a DNT signal is detected and no prior consent choice has been recorded, we will treat your visit as if you selected "Reject" on the consent banner — meaning no analytics cookies will be loaded.

8. Data Retention

The retention periods for each storage item and cookie are listed in the tables in Section 4. As a general principle:

  • localStorage items persist indefinitely on your device until you manually clear them or until our JavaScript code removes them (for example, on logout). They are not subject to automatic browser expiry.
  • Session HTTP cookies are automatically deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent HTTP cookies remain on your device until their defined expiry date is reached or until you delete them.
  • Google Analytics retains anonymised usage data in its own systems for the period set in our GA4 property settings (typically 14 months). This data does not contain your name or direct identifiers.
